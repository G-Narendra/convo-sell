

1. Problem Statement
1.1  The Broken Customer Interaction Model
In most physical and digital retail environments — restaurants, hotels, retail stores — customer interaction is handled by human staff who are inconsistent, unavailable, emotionally reactive, and unable to process customer signals at scale. The result is a systematic mismatch between what customers actually want and what they are offered.

1.3  Why Existing Solutions Are Insufficient
Static Digital Menus (QR codes): Display information but do not converse, influence, or capture mood. Purely passive.
Chatbots: Rule-based, not emotion-aware. Cannot adapt tone, detect frustration, or personalise in real time.
Loyalty Apps: Capture historical preferences but do not influence in-the-moment. Cannot detect live emotional state.
Human Staff Training: Expensive, inconsistent, and not scalable. The best-trained waiter still cannot process multiple emotional signals simultaneously.


2. Proposed Solution: ConvoSell AI
2.1  Core Concept
ConvoSell AI is a real-time, emotionally intelligent conversational agent deployed on a physical screen interface at the point of customer interaction. It reads the customer's mood and communication style through natural language and tonal analysis, then adapts its personality, recommendations, and persuasion strategy in real time to maximise both customer satisfaction and business revenue — simultaneously.


2.2  The Five Core Capabilities
Capability 1: Real-Time Mood & Tone Detection
Using NLP-based sentiment analysis and affective computing models, the system classifies the customer's emotional state on each conversational turn. States tracked include: happy/excited, neutral/browsing, tired/low-energy, confused/overwhelmed, impatient/angry, and indecisive. The detected mood continuously updates a Mood State Vector that drives all downstream recommendations and tone adaptation.

Capability 2: Preference & Dietary Profiling
Within the first 2-3 turns, the AI identifies key customer attributes: vegetarian/non-vegetarian/vegan status, spice tolerance, portion preference, allergens, cuisine preference, and budget signals. These are stored in a Session Context Object and used to filter and rank menu recommendations.

Capability 3: Influence-Optimised Recommendation Engine
The AI selects dishes using a multi-objective ranking function that balances the customer's detected preferences with the business's profit margin targets and inventory levels. High-margin items are surfaced with emotionally resonant descriptions tailored to the customer's current mood. For an excited customer: 'This is our chef's showstopper dish.' For a tired customer: 'This is our most comforting, no-fuss favourite — perfect when you want something reliably great.'

Capability 4: Structured Micro-Preference Kitchen Orders
Once the customer finalises their order, the AI generates a structured JSON order payload that includes not just the items ordered, but all micro-preferences captured during conversation: spice level (1–5 scale), salt preference, cooking style, allergy flags, portion size, special requests, and priority/urgency level. This is transmitted directly to a kitchen display system or POS terminal.

Capability 5: Engagement Bridge (Wait Time AI)
After the order is placed, the AI transitions from a sales mode to an engagement mode. It continues conversation — offering entertainment, trivia, conversation topics, or ambient brand storytelling — keeping the customer emotionally positive during wait time. At kitchen completion, it delivers a personalised, mood-matched notification: 'Your Paneer Tikka is on its way — we hope it's exactly what you needed tonight.'

5. Machine Learning Models & Research Foundation
5.1  Model Stack
Sentiment & Emotion Detection: Fine-tuned DistilBERT on GoEmotions dataset (Demszky et al., 2020). Classifies 27 emotion categories reduced to 5 actionable mood states. Real-time inference < 80ms per turn.
Dialogue Generation: Instruction-tuned LLM (LLaMA 3 8B or GPT-4o) with a mood-indexed system prompt that dynamically adjusts tone, vocabulary complexity, and persuasion intensity.
Recommendation Ranking: Multi-objective optimisation function: Score = α × PreferenceMatch + β × ProfitMargin + γ × InventoryWeight + δ × SocialProofScore. Weights (α, β, γ, δ) configurable per business context.
Wait Time Engagement: Retrieval-augmented module using restaurant-specific knowledge base (dish stories, chef background, brand content) to generate contextually relevant conversation.

5.2  Academic Grounding
Affective Computing: Picard (1997) — foundational framework for emotion-aware machine systems. Applied here to real-time tone adaptation in commercial contexts.
Persuasion & Framing Theory: Cialdini (2001) social proof, scarcity, and liking principles formalised as prompt engineering templates indexed to mood state.
Conversational AI Design: Grice's Maxims of cooperative communication — AI responses are calibrated to be relevant, appropriately brief, and truthful.
Multi-Objective Optimisation: Pareto-efficient ranking between customer satisfaction score and business profit margin — formalised as a constrained optimisation problem.

7. Innovation Statement
ConvoSell AI is novel not because it applies NLP to customer service — that exists. It is novel because it treats mood-state as a first-class variable in both the recommendation algorithm and the revenue function, and because it integrates the full loop from conversation to kitchen order to post-service engagement in a single coherent AI agent. The dual-objective design — customer satisfaction AND business profit as simultaneous optimisation targets — reflects an understanding that these are not competing goals, but compounding ones.
